# SCP: Dead Letter Protocol — Steam Capsule Art Guide

Compiled from: Valve's Steamworks documentation, presskit.gg 2026 guide,
and progamemarketing's capsule art review (Chris Zukowski). This document
is designed to inform nanobanana image prompts for DLP capsule creation.

## 2. Valve's Content Rules (Strict)

**Allowed:**
- Game artwork
- Game title / logotype
- Official subtitle

**Banned (violating = ineligible for Steam featuring/sales):**
- Review scores or press quotes
- Award logos or names
- Discount text ("On Sale Now", "50% Off")
- Cross-promotional imagery
- Any miscellaneous marketing text

## 3. The Core Principles (What Makes Capsules Perform)

### 3.1 — The One-Second Rule
Players scrolling the Discovery Queue give you approximately one second.
Your capsule must communicate genre instantly through visual symbols alone.
It works at the "subbrain level" — viewers don't analyze, they pattern-match.

### 3.2 — Genre Symbology
Every genre has "secret symbols" that instantly communicate what kind of game
it is. You are NOT trying to reinvent capsules. You are trying to match the
visual language players already recognize.

**Horror game symbols (our genre — "the easiest capsule to make"):**
- A scary/ominous face or figure
- Dark, high-contrast lighting
- Blood, scratches, distortion effects
- White text over dark imagery
- Shadowy bust shot with glowing eyes
- Unsettling close-up with simple typography

**Strategy/tactical symbols:**
- Full-body character surveying a landscape
- Map or terrain elements below the character
- Compass, grid, or strategic overlay elements

**Roguelike/mystery symbols:**
- Dice (for dice-based), cards (for card-based) — but NOT for DLP
- Keys, locks, magnifying glasses (mystery/investigation)
- Blood splatter + text (narrative mystery)

**For DLP specifically, the symbols to consider:**
- SCP redaction bars (black bars) — instantly recognizable to SCP fans
- A classified document aesthetic
- An ominous creature/anomaly partially obscured
- A lone operative/agent figure
- Facility/institutional setting (corridors, containment cells)
- Redacted text or distortion overlays
- The SCP Foundation logo or similar bureaucratic insignia

### 3.3 — The Squint Test
Squint your eyes or view the capsule at thumbnail size. If the key elements
(character, title, mood) disappear into mush, the contrast is too low.
High-performing capsules have strong silhouettes and hard contrast lines
that read even when blurred.

### 3.4 — The 120x45 Pixel Test
The Small Capsule auto-generates a 120x45 version. Your title MUST be
readable at this size. Design for the smallest size first. At this resolution,
busy details become illegible noise.

### 3.5 — Single Focal Point
Avoid collages. Commit to ONE hero character, ONE environment, or ONE
emotional tone. Multiple competing elements create visual noise at small sizes.
"Pick one. They're all cool. You just got to pick one."

### 3.6 — Professional Quality Bar
The capsule's primary job is proving you're not a hobbyist. Steam shoppers
are subconsciously filtering out amateur-looking games. The capsule must
clear the "this wasn't made by your girlfriend" bar.

## 4. Typography Rules

### 4.1 — The Golden Rules of Capsule Text
1. **Two different text sizes** — vary the height (big title word, smaller subtitle)
2. **White text is cool** — "If you don't know what to do, just make it white. No gradients, no gloss, no glow. Just white. That shows confidence."
3. **Modify a vowel** — Turn one letter into a genre symbol (the O into a skull, the A into an ace, etc.). This is the #1 indicator of professional capsule text.
4. **Add a decorator** — a small visual element woven into or around the text (wisps, a weapon, a symbol)
5. **Vary letter positioning** — pull a letter down, shift something up. Not robotic uniform placement.

### 4.2 — Dead Giveaways of Amateur Text (avoid ALL of these)
- All caps, all same size, all same font
- No vowel modification or decorators
- Gradients, gloss, shine, glow effects on text
- Colored text instead of white (unless specifically styled by a professional)
- Illegible decorative/gothic fonts
- Text that reads as the wrong word due to creative letter substitution

### 4.3 — For DLP's Title Text
"SCP: Dead Letter Protocol" offers opportunities:
- The **O** in "Protocol" could incorporate a redaction bar, a containment symbol, or the SCP logo
- The **P** in "Protocol" could have a subtle drip or distortion
- "SCP" as a prefix naturally reads as classified/institutional
- Consider a classified-document font treatment: clean, institutional, slightly distressed
- Two sizes: "SCP:" smaller/above, "Dead Letter Protocol" larger/below
- White text on dark background = professional + horror appropriate

## 5. Composition Strategies

### 5.1 — The Bust Shot (recommended for DLP)
The most cost-effective and proven capsule composition:
- Character from chest/shoulders up
- Simple textured background (not a full environment)
- Title text overlaid
- One genre symbol worked into text or held by character

This works especially well for horror: "shadowy bust, glowing eyes, white text."
Hades uses this. Icarus uses this. Most successful horror games use this.

### 5.2 — Horror-Specific Composition
"Horror is the easiest capsule to make. You just put a scary face and some
white text over it and you are good to go."

Best horror capsule formula:
1. Dark, high-contrast background
2. An ominous face/figure (partially obscured works great for mystery)
3. Clean white title text
4. One or two subtle symbols (blood, scratches, distortion)

### 5.3 — Color Strategy
- Study comparable games and borrow their color palette
- Horror games: dark backgrounds (black, deep blue, deep red)
- High contrast between subject and background
- Don't use warm/bright colors that read as "wholesome" or "cozy"
- The emotional tone of your colors must match your genre

### 5.4 — Silhouette and Contrast
- Characters must read as clear silhouettes even at small sizes
- Dark characters on dark backgrounds disappear — use rim lighting or contrast
- Action poses must be readable in silhouette (side profile > facing camera)
- "Do the squint test" — if it's mush when you squint, increase contrast

### 5.5 — What NOT to Do
- Don't use a screenshot of the game as your capsule
- Don't pack every detail about your game into one image
- Don't use multiple competing art styles in one capsule
- Don't make the character face away from the viewer or perform ambiguous actions
- Don't put critical visual elements at the edges where they get cropped

## 6. Comparable Horror Game Capsule Patterns

Based on successful horror games in DLP's competitive space:

**World of Horror:** High contrast black and white, stylized character bust,
clean title text, retro/distorted aesthetic

**Darkest Dungeon:** Dark reds and blacks, torchlit character silhouettes,
gothic but readable title text, oppressive mood

**Lobotomy Corporation:** Clean institutional aesthetic, creature/monster
focal point, bright accent colors against dark background

**Inscryption:** Dark forest/cabin tones, single mysterious focal point,
clean white title text, psychological dread over gore

**Common patterns across all:**
- Dark color palettes (blacks, deep reds, desaturated tones)
- Single character or creature as focal point
- Clean, readable title text (usually white or light)
- Mood > literal depiction (evoke dread, don't show every mechanic)
- High contrast that reads at thumbnail size

## 7. DLP Capsule Strategy Recommendations

### Visual Identity to Convey
- **Genre:** Horror + Strategy + Roguelike
- **Setting:** SCP facility (institutional, clinical, things-going-wrong)
- **Tone:** Psychological dread, not gore. Analog horror aesthetic.
- **Art style:** Realistic 2D, appears 3D. Dark, high-fidelity.

### Recommended Approach
**Primary composition:** Bust shot of an SCP creature or operative, partially
obscured by redaction bars or facility elements. Dark background with
institutional/facility texture. Clean white title text with one modified letter
(the O in Protocol as an SCP containment symbol, or redaction bars worked
into the text).

**Key symbols to incorporate (pick 1-2):**
- Black redaction bars (THE iconic SCP visual — instantly recognizable)
- A partially revealed creature face behind bars/glass
- Classified document texture or stamp
- Facility corridor or containment cell elements
- A lone operative silhouette facing something unseen

**Color palette:**
- Primary: Deep blacks, charcoal grays
- Accent: Clinical white, warning red, containment amber
- Avoid: Bright colors, warm tones, anything that reads "cozy" or "action"

### What the Capsule Must NOT Communicate
- Not a card game / deckbuilder (no cards visible)
- Not a pixel art game (realistic art style must be evident)
- Not a jumpscare horror game (psychological, not shock)
- Not a management sim (despite facility setting)
- Not anime-styled

### Small Capsule Strategy
Design a dedicated Small Capsule version. Don't auto-scale the Header.
At 462x174, strip background detail and fill the frame with the title.
The SCP prefix + "Dead Letter Protocol" in professional typography with
one redaction-bar element is enough to communicate horror + SCP + mystery.

## 8. Nanobanana Prompt Considerations

When generating reference art with nanobanana, these elements should guide prompts:

**Style keywords:** realistic digital art, dark atmospheric, high contrast,
cinematic lighting, institutional horror, analog horror aesthetic, SCP
Foundation, classified document, containment facility

**Composition keywords:** bust shot, centered character, dark background,
dramatic lighting, rim light, silhouette contrast, negative space for title text

**Mood keywords:** psychological dread, clinical horror, something is wrong,
containment breach, institutional decay, ominous, foreboding

**Avoid in prompts:** pixel art, anime, cartoon, bright colors, action pose,
cards, deckbuilder, cute, wholesome, gore, jumpscares, over-detailed background

**Technical notes:**
- Leave clear space for title text overlay (usually upper third or lower third)
- Ensure the focal point reads at thumbnail size (strong silhouette)
- Dark backgrounds with a single lit subject photograph well at all capsule sizes
- Redaction bars / classified overlays can be composited after generation
